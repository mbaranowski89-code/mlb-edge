SCORECARD METRICS
STEAM %
How much Pinnacle's vig-removed fair probability moved toward this team since the opening line was set. Positive means sharp (professional) money bet this team, forcing Pinnacle to adjust. This is the primary signal — sharp money found value here first.
Steam = Pinnacle current fair − Pinnacle opening fair
0–1.4% — Weak, minimal score contribution
1.5–2.9% — Meaningful steam
3%+ — Significant sharp action · 3.5%+ = max score contribution (30 pts)
LAG WINDOW %
How much of the retail pricing opportunity created by Pinnacle's steam move is still open and exploitable. When Pinnacle moves, retail books take time to catch up. This measures what percentage of that gap remains. Only meaningful when steam exists.
Lag Window = (Retail vig-removed fair − Pinnacle current fair) ÷ Steam shift × 100
0–24% — Nearly closed, likely too late
25–59% — Closing, act soon
60–100% — Wide open
MODEL GAP %
How far behind the retail book's probability model is vs Pinnacle's, with vig removed from both sides first. Positive means retail is behind Pinnacle — their model hasn't caught up to where sharp money has moved the true probability. This is good for you. Negative means retail is ahead of Pinnacle — they've overpriced this team.
Model Gap = Pinnacle vig-removed fair − Retail vig-removed fair
Negative — Retail ahead of Pinnacle (bad)
0–1.9% — Retail slightly behind
2%+ — Retail meaningfully behind Pinnacle
BET EDGE %
Direct profitability of the bet assuming Pinnacle's fair probability is correct. Compares Pinnacle's vig-removed fair against your break-even probability at the retail price. Positive means the bet is profitable if Pinnacle is right.
Bet Edge = Pinnacle vig-removed fair − Break-even at retail odds Break-even = |odds| ÷ (|odds| + 100) [for favorites] Break-even = 100 ÷ (odds + 100) [for underdogs]
Negative — Not profitable at retail price
0–1.9% — Marginal edge
2%+ — Good edge · 3%+ = max score contribution (40 pts)
ODDS GAP
The best available retail odds vs Pinnacle's fair probability expressed as American odds (zero-vig equivalent). Shows the price difference in familiar betting terms. Positive means you're getting a better price than what Pinnacle would charge at zero vig.
Pinnacle fair odds = convert vig-removed fair probability back to American odds Odds Gap = Retail odds − Pinnacle fair odds
0–4 pts — Minimal difference
5–9 pts — Meaningful lag
10+ pts — Significant retail lag

VIG & PROBABILITY MATH
WHAT IS VIG?
The sportsbook's built-in profit margin. Both sides of a game are priced so the combined implied probabilities exceed 100%. The excess is vig. A standard -110/-110 line has ~4.76% vig. Pinnacle charges ~2–3% on MLB — the lowest of any major book, making their lines the most accurate reflection of true probability.
Vig = (Away implied + Home implied) − 100% Example: -110/-110 → 52.38% + 52.38% = 104.76% → Vig = 4.76%
CONVERTING ODDS TO IMPLIED PROBABILITY
American odds can be converted to the probability the book is implying for each outcome.
Favorite (negative odds): |odds| ÷ (|odds| + 100) Example: -130 → 130 ÷ 230 = 56.52% Underdog (positive odds): 100 ÷ (odds + 100) Example: +110 → 100 ÷ 210 = 47.62%
REMOVING THE VIG (ADDITIVE METHOD)
Dividing each side's implied probability by the total removes the book's profit margin and gives the "true" probability each side wins according to that book's model.
Away fair = Away implied ÷ (Away implied + Home implied) Home fair = Home implied ÷ (Away implied + Home implied) Example: SF -115 / CIN +104 SF implied = 115 ÷ 215 = 53.49% CIN implied = 100 ÷ 204 = 49.02% Total = 102.51% SF fair = 53.49 ÷ 102.51 = 52.18% CIN fair = 49.02 ÷ 102.51 = 47.82%
PERFORMANCE TRACKING
CLOSING LINE VALUE (CLV)
The odds you got vs the closing line (final odds before game start). Negative CLV means you got a better price than where the line closed — you beat the market. Consistently negative CLV over many bets is the strongest indicator of a real edge, more reliable than win rate over small samples.
CLV = Closing implied probability − Your implied probability Negative = you got the better price (good)
KELLY SIZING
Mathematically optimal bet size given your edge and bankroll. The app uses Quarter Kelly by default — this reduces variance significantly while preserving most of the mathematical advantage. Hard cap of $10 per bet is recommended until 100+ bets are logged.
Full Kelly = (Edge × Bankroll) ÷ Decimal odds Quarter Kelly = Full Kelly × 0.25
WHY PINNACLE?
Pinnacle accepts sharp (professional) bettors without limiting them. When their line is wrong, sharp bettors immediately correct it. This continuous pressure makes Pinnacle's closing line one of the most accurate probability estimates available — more accurate than ESPN's model, public consensus, or other retail books. Beating Pinnacle's closing line consistently means you're finding real edges.
